Research Interests

My research interests integrates statistical modeling with theoretical foundations, machine learning, and data-driven insights to transform complex data into actionable solutions for real-world challenges. My past and ongoning projects encompasses the following domains:
  • Multimodal large-scale spatio-temporal data modeling with efficient Bayesian computational algorithms.
  • Irregular functional data analysis with applications in free-lving acceleration data.
  • Clinical trial design for ordinal responses data.
  • Psychometric scale development and validation.
Research Overview: My Ph.D. focus on advancing functional data analysis for irregular (data with shape constraints, or varying covariance structures), high-dimensional datasets, emphasizing applications in gait data acquired from individuals with neurodegenerative diseases via wearable devices. During this period, I conducted an efficient clinical trail design for comparisions of treatments with ordinal responses with sequential monitoring and response-adaptive randomization under the supervisor Prof. Siu Hung Cheung. I co-developed a novel scale Neuo-11 with the Department of Neurology, Shenzhen People's Hospital and conducted the structural data analysis and statistically validated the scale for somatic symptom disorder screening.
